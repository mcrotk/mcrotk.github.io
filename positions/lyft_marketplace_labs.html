Lyft Marketplace Labs

Lyft has grown dramatically from a small startup fiercely fighting for survival in the nascent ridesharing space to a major player in transportation tech anticipating the transition to becoming a public company. It has made bold pushes into long-term bets like autonomous vehicles (AVs), and ambitions to be a key partner with cities in reinventing urban transportation.

A tactical, fast-iteration approach to science development served Lyft well in its early years; however, to be competitive against other tech giants in the transportation space and achieve our ambitious growth objectives over the long term, we also need to develop complementary strategic, long-run science capabilities. This is the purpose of Marketplace Labs.

Mission Statement

Marketplace Labs' mission is to firmly establish Lyft as the global scientific and research leader in building and operating transportation marketplaces at scale, from rideshare to AVs to city-scale multimodal systems. Marketplace Labs accomplishes this objective by partnering with internal and external collaborators to bring the latest scientific knowledge to bear on Lyft's core technical and business challenges.

Roles

Marketplace Labs serves three major roles:

Acceleration

Accelerating the work of other Lyft research science and product groups by:

  • Providing additional expertise, advice and collaboration on difficult science problems;
  • Serving as a swing resource to incubate new ideas and perform exploratory applied research to better understand and solve strategic problems and vet potential solutions;
  • Helping to coordinate and align the efforts of research science related to marketplace issues that cut across the company.

Incubation

Conducting our own research and thought leadership on strategic long-term challenges facing Lyft, collaborating closely with existing marketplace science and product groups and (where appropriate) outside academic experts.

Outreach

Serving as a home for visiting scientists from academia and generally helping to facilitate interaction between Lyft scientists and the academic research community. The aim of this interaction is to help inject new skills and knowledge into Lyft research science, strengthen the Lyft brand throughout the scientific world, and share the great science work we do with external scientific colleagues, including participating in published research.

Engagement

Engagement philosophy

Marketplace Labs' philosophy is to serve as a shared resource that partners closely with other teams in Lyft who own the relevant problem areas, not as an isolated ivory tower tossing ideas over the wall. As such, lab problem areas and projects are anchored on the core strategic needs of Lyft's business, albeit not bound entirely by today's issues and constraints. At the same time, by having an exploratory, applied research mandate and a longer-term focus, the labs team can take on higher risk and more open-ended problems that are difficult to address under the framework of existing, execution-oriented teams.

Engagement methodology

The labs engagement method consists of two phases: initial research and pilot development. Each phase involves two teams, the labs team and product-owner team. During the research phase, product-owner teams embed members on the labs team to aid with exploratory research, ideation and prototype development. The target time allocation is approximately 80% labs team time and 20% product-owner team time. If the research phase proves promising, the project moves to the pilot development phase.

During this second phase, members of the labs team embed on the product-owner teams to assist in turning the prototype into production. The target time allocation in this second phase is approximately 80% product-owner team time and 20% labs team time. Once the product pilot is validated, the labs team disengages, serving only as a consulting resource as needed for further problem solving and enhancements.

This engagement methodology has multiple goals. The first is to ensure that labs project are rooted in product-owner team needs and also benefit from their problem domain experience. It also ensures that knowledge and innovations developed in the research phase are transferred effectively to the project-owner teams in the development phase. Lastly, it helps broaden the participation of Lyft research scientists in the labs work, so that all research scientists can benefit from working on labs research projects and learning from labs senior researchers.
